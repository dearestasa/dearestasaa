Greetings, you!

Green, brown, and dirty white

Lo, feast thine eyes upon this chamber—an enclave of subdued opulence and hushed reverie. Cloaked in rich umber walls and shadowed by the great boughs beyond yon glass, the room lies as though carved from the very marrow of the earth. Vast windows, like portals to a forgotten woodland, allow the green hush of nature to press close against the soul of the house, as if the forest doth breathe upon the glass. At the room's heart, a table of noble design doth rest, flanked by four austere chairs clad in leather—aged and proud. Above it, a mural stretches long and low, bearing the visage of mountains undulating like whispered secrets—earth’s poetry writ in hue and curve. The carpet, pale as morning fog, doth hush each footfall, whilst to the side, cushioned seats in plaid repose with a quiet confidence, as though awaiting a gathering not of men, but of memories. This be not a mere dining hall, but a sanctum where time doth slow, where the whisper of leaves and the silence of stone hold sway. A place wherein each shadow sings and every line tells the tale of a house both solemn and sublime.
